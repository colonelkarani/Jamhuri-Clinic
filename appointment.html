Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about our orthopaedic services and appointment process.

Please bring:

  • Your insurance card and photo ID
  • Completed intake forms (if not submitted online)
  • Medical records related to your condition
  • List of current medications and supplements
  • Recent imaging studies (X-rays, MRIs) on CD or USB
  • Comfortable clothing to allow examination of affected area

We prioritize urgent cases and typically offer appointments within 48 hours for acute injuries or pain. Routine consultations are usually scheduled within 5-7 days. Check our online booking system for real-time availability across all providers.

We participate with most major insurance plans including Medicare, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Aetna, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, and Tricare. Our team will verify your benefits prior to your visit and explain any out-of-pocket costs. We also offer self-pay options and payment plans for patients without insurance coverage.

We maintain strict infection control protocols including:

  • HEPA air filtration in all clinical areas
  • Enhanced cleaning between patients
  • Optional telehealth appointments
  • Screening for symptoms upon arrival
  • Masking requirements aligned with CDC guidelines
  • Vaccinated clinical staff

Recovery varies by procedure and individual factors:

  • Knee arthroscopy: 4-6 weeks for full recovery
  • Shoulder arthroscopy: 6-8 weeks typically
  • Wrist/hand procedures: 3-4 weeks usually
  • Small joint procedures: 2-3 weeks commonly

Most patients resume light activities within 2-3 days and office work within a week. Your surgeon will provide a personalized recovery plan.
